About A.S.M.R.

What is A.S.M.R.?

Autonomous sensory meridian response. Autonomous means it is without control, sensory means related to senses, meridian means a climax, and response means it is triggered by something. Ultimately ASMR is a response, usually characterized by tingling and euphoric sensations, to specific stimuli. These stimuli, also known as triggers, are usually auditory or visual and can be encountered in everyday life. For example hearing rain, brushing hair, or chewing food.

What is the science behind it?

Research into the effects of ASMR triggers in the brain are only recently being investigated. Scientists are curious to see if the ASMR experience can shed insight into how the brain practices the sensations of pain and relaxation.

Why is it relevant now?

ASMR was an internet based occurrence that came onto the scene a mere seven years ago. It is just now becoming known to mainstream culture and is attracting a wide audience who seek it due to the pleasurable and relaxing sensations it provides. Although many of these sounds and visuals have been found before in daily life, they are now accessible on the web making ASMR easier to experience.
